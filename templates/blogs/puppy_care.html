How to Take Care of Your Puppy and Feed Them

üìÖ Published: March 15, 2025 ‚è±Ô∏è 15 min read üè∑Ô∏è Nutrition, Puppy Care

The first few weeks and months of a puppy's life are absolutely critical for their development, health, and future well-being. Proper nutrition and care during this period lay the foundation for a long, healthy, and happy life. At MrigaAayuvets, we understand that new puppy parents often feel overwhelmed by the responsibility of caring for their tiny new family member.

This comprehensive guide will walk you through everything you need to know about feeding and caring for your puppy, from their first days to their first birthday and beyond.

The Critical First Week

The first week of a puppy's life is indeed the most critical period for survival. During this time, puppies are completely dependent on their mother for warmth, nutrition, and even basic bodily functions.

What Happens in the First Week

  • Days 1-3: Puppies should nurse within the first few hours of birth to receive colostrum, which contains essential antibodies
  • Days 4-7: Puppies should gain weight daily and sleep most of the time
  • Eyes and ears: Remain closed during this period
  • Temperature regulation: Puppies cannot regulate their body temperature and rely on their mother and littermates

‚ö†Ô∏è Emergency Signs in Newborn Puppies:

  • Not gaining weight or losing weight
  • Constant crying or lethargy
  • Difficulty nursing
  • Cold to the touch
  • Diarrhea or lack of urination

Contact your veterinarian immediately if you notice any of these signs.

Choosing the Right Puppy Food

Not all puppy foods are created equal. The quality of nutrition you provide during the puppy stage will impact your dog's health throughout their life.

What to Look for in Puppy Food

  • AAFCO certification: Look for foods that meet AAFCO (Association of American Feed Control Officials) standards
  • High-quality protein: Should be the first ingredient (chicken, lamb, fish)
  • DHA: Essential fatty acid for brain and eye development
  • Appropriate calcium levels: For proper bone development
  • No artificial preservatives: Avoid BHA, BHT, and ethoxyquin

üí° Feeding Tips for Success:

  • Stick to a consistent feeding schedule
  • Measure portions according to package guidelines
  • Use the same bowl and feeding location
  • Remove uneaten food after 20-30 minutes
  • Always provide fresh water

Foods to Avoid

Many human foods are toxic to puppies and dogs. Never feed your puppy:

  • Chocolate (especially dark chocolate)
  • Grapes and raisins
  • Onions and garlic
  • Xylitol (artificial sweetener)
  • Avocado
  • Cooked bones (can splinter)
  • Alcohol
  • Caffeine

The Weaning Process (4-8 weeks)

Weaning is the gradual transition from mother's milk to solid food. This process should be done slowly and carefully.

Step-by-Step Weaning Guide

  1. Week 4: Introduce "puppy mush" - high-quality puppy food mixed with warm water or puppy milk replacer
  2. Week 5: Gradually reduce the liquid, making the food thicker
  3. Week 6: Offer mostly solid food with minimal liquid
  4. Week 7-8: Puppies should be eating solid food independently

Portion Control and Weight Management

Overfeeding is just as dangerous as underfeeding. Overweight puppies are more prone to joint problems and other health issues later in life.

üí° How to Determine Proper Portions:

  • Follow feeding guidelines on puppy food packaging
  • Adjust based on your puppy's body condition
  • You should be able to feel your puppy's ribs without pressing hard
  • Your puppy should have a visible waist when viewed from above
  • Consult your veterinarian for personalized recommendations

Hydration and Water Needs

Puppies need constant access to fresh, clean water. Young puppies get most of their hydration from mother's milk, but as they wean, water becomes increasingly important.

Water Guidelines

  • Provide fresh water daily in clean bowls
  • Puppies need about ¬Ω to 1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily
  • Monitor water intake - excessive drinking can indicate health problems
  • Clean water bowls regularly to prevent bacteria growth
  • Consider multiple water stations in larger homes

Essential Care Beyond Feeding

Proper nutrition is just one aspect of puppy care. Here are other critical areas that require attention:

Vaccination Schedule

Puppies need a series of vaccinations to protect them from serious diseases. Work with your veterinarian to establish a vaccination schedule.

Typical vaccination timeline:

  • 6-8 weeks: First DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza)
  • 10-12 weeks: Second DHPP, first Rabies
  • 14-16 weeks: Third DHPP
  • 12-16 weeks: Rabies vaccination

Socialization (3-14 weeks)

The socialization period is critical for developing a well-adjusted adult dog. Expose your puppy to:

  • Different people (children, adults, seniors)
  • Various sounds and environments
  • Other vaccinated, friendly dogs
  • Different surfaces and textures
  • Car rides and handling

‚ö†Ô∏è Socialization Safety:

Until your puppy is fully vaccinated, avoid areas with unknown dogs or high traffic areas like dog parks. Focus on controlled socialization with known, healthy, vaccinated dogs.

House Training

Start house training immediately when you bring your puppy home:

  • Take your puppy outside frequently (every 1-2 hours)
  • Always go outside after meals, naps, and play
  • Praise and reward successful outdoor elimination
  • Never punish accidents - clean thoroughly and try again
  • Use a consistent command like "go potty"

Sleep and Rest

Puppies need 18-20 hours of sleep per day for proper development. Provide:

  • A comfortable, quiet sleeping area
  • Consistent bedtime routine
  • Crate training for security and house training
  • Undisturbed nap times

Common Feeding Problems and Solutions

Picky Eating

Some puppies may be finicky eaters. Try these solutions:

  • Stick to a consistent feeding schedule
  • Remove uneaten food after 20 minutes
  • Avoid giving treats between meals
  • Try warming the food slightly
  • Mix in a small amount of low-sodium chicken broth

Eating Too Fast

Fast eating can lead to choking or bloat. Solutions include:

  • Use a slow-feeder bowl
  • Spread food on a large, flat surface
  • Feed smaller, more frequent meals
  • Add obstacles like tennis balls to the food bowl

Digestive Upset

Puppies have sensitive stomachs. Signs of digestive issues include:

  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Loss of appetite
  • Lethargy
  • Abdominal pain

üí° When to Call Your Veterinarian:

  • Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Signs of dehydration
  • Loss of appetite for more than 24 hours
  • Lethargy or weakness
  • Any concerning behavior changes

Conclusion

Caring for a puppy requires dedication, consistency, and patience, but the rewards are immeasurable. The first few months of your puppy's life will set the foundation for their entire future. By providing proper nutrition, consistent care, and lots of love, you'll help your puppy grow into a healthy, happy, well-adjusted adult dog.

Remember that every puppy is unique, and what works for one may not work for another. Don't hesitate to consult with your veterinarian at MrigaAayuvets if you have questions or concerns about your puppy's development, feeding, or behavior.
